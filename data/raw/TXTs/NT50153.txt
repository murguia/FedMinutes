Money market review. Today's review consisted of comments by 
Mr. Kelty on the Government securities market, including the current 
Treasury financing, and by Mr. Fry on current and prospective bank 
credit developments. Copies of the tables and charts distributed for 
the purpose of the review have been placed in the Board's files. 
Members of the staff other than Messrs. Kenyon, Brill, Hackley, 
Solomon (Examinations), Sammons, Dahl, and Forrestal then withdrew.

The following actions were taken subject to ratification at the 
next meeting of the Board at which a quorum was present: 
Discount rates. The establishment without change by the Federal 
Reserve Bank of Atlanta on October 3 and by the Federal Reserve Banks 
of New York, Philadelphia, Chicago, Kansas City, and San Francisco on 
October 5, 1967, of the rates on discounts and advances in their exist-
ing schedules was approved unanimously, with the understanding that 
appropriate advice would be sent to those Banks. 
Call for condition reports. Under the procedure prescribed by 
statute the close of business Wednesday, October 4, 1967, had been 
selected as the date for the third call for reports of condition to 
be made by insured banks within the calendar year 1967. The call was 
to be announced at 9:00 a.m., Tuesday, October 10. 
In accordance with the usual practice a telegram was sent to 
all Federal Reserve Banks on October 5 advising that a call for reports 
of condition by State member banks as of the close of business October 4 
should be made at a specified time on forms furnished with the Board's 
letter of September 22, 1967. 
The sending of the telegram was ratified by unanimous vote. 
Technical assistance (Item No. 1). Unanimous approval was 
given to a letter to the Federal Reserve Bank of New York (copy attached 
as Item No. 1) relating to arrangements made to provide technical assis-
tance to the Bank of Guyana on internal audit and security matters. 
The meeting then adjourned.

Dear Mr. Tarver: 
Acting under delegated authority, the Director of the 
Division of Personnel Administration has approved the request con-
tained in your letter of October 3, 1967, for the appointments of 
Messrs. James N. Augustine and James D. Murphy as Federal Reserve 
Agent's Representatives at the Nashville Branch. 
This approval is given with the understanding that Messrs. 
Augustine and Murphy will be solely responsible to the Federal Reserve 
Agent and the Board of Governors for the proper performance of their 
duties, except that, during the absence or disability of the Federal 
Reserve Agent or a vacancy in that office, their responsibilities will 
be to the Assistant Federal Reserve Agent and the Board of Governors. 
When not engaged in the performance of their duties as 
Federal Reserve Agent's Representatives, Messrs. Augustine and Murphy 
way, with the approval of the Federal Reserve Agent and the President, 
perform such work for the Bank as will not be inconsistent with their 
duties as Federal Reserve Agent's Representatives. 
It will be appreciated if Messrs. Augustine and Murphy are 
fully informed of the importance of their responsibilities as members 
°f the staff of the Federal Reserve Agent and the need for maintenance 
of independence from the operations of the Bank in the discharge of 
these responsibilities. 
Please have Messrs. Augustine and Murphy execute the usual 
Oath of Office which should be forwarded to the Board of Governors. 
Very truly yours, 
(Signed) Kenneth A. Kenyon
