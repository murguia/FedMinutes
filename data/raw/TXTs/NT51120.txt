Dear President Coldwell: 
The Board has considered the recommendation, contained in 
your letter of September 11, that fully collateralized 
large-denomination 
cD's issued to public bodies be exempted from marginal reserve 
requirements. 
While the Board understands the basis for the proposal, it 
has decided, on the basis of the following factors, not to grant such 
a n exemption. 
First, many banks already have in their portfolio 
securities that could be used as collateral for public deposits; 
additional CD funds, in those cases, could be used to make loans. 
Second, even in those cases where a bank would have to acquire collat-
eral securities in the market, the result would be a net expansion in 
hank credit. 
Potential lenders that might otherwise have purchased 
the State and local government securities would then have funds that 
eould be lent elsewhere — including to business firms. 
Finally, the 
Board is concerned that such an action could prompt possible requests 
to reduce reserve requirements on any deposit which is fully collat-
e r a l i 2 e d > including for exaifiple Treasury deposits. 
While the Board concluded, on balance, not to grant the 
requested exemption, it nevertheless appreciated your action in 
bringing this matter to its attention. 
Sincerely, 
Chester B. Feldberg

Guidelines for emergency lending to 
nonmember c o n m e r c i a l banks and to 
savings banks that are not members of the 
Federal Home Loan Bank System 
A decision to extend emergency credit should be based upon the probable 
consequences of the failure to obtain Reserve Bank credit and the impact 
°f any resulting bank failure on the community or the economy. 
It would 
he most unusual for the failure of a single institution or small group of 
institutions to have such significant repercussions as to justify Federal 
Reserve action. 
However, in exceptional and unusual cases, credit may be 
extended to a solvent nonmember bank unable to obtain assistance elsewhere 
in order to prevent the failure of the bank, provided that the failure, in 
the Reserve Bank's judgment, would impose a serious hardship on the com-
munity or economy, or would seriously affect the condition of other banks. 
A 
Credit should be extended to nonmember banks only if all reasonable 
alternative sources of credit have been exhausted, and only if the bank has 
made all reasonable and appropriate adjustments in its investment portfolio 
an<* lending practices. 
Emergency credit should be made available to a nonmember bank only after 
consultation with the Board and the responsible regional Federal and/or 
State bank supervisory agencies. 
A Federal Reserve Bank should not make a 
loan to a nonmember bank against the wishes of the appropriate supervisory 
agency without prior consultation on that matter with the Board of Governors, 
n°r should it be obligated to lend to nonmember banks on an emergency basis 
Merely because of a request by such agency. 
A nonmember bank granted emergency credit will be expected to arrange 
Promptly for all reasonable and orderly adjustments of its position in
