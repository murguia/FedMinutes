among members of the Board and Governor Bucher agreed to indicate such

Mortgage guaranty insurance. Against the background of a memo-
randum from the Banking Markets Section and the Division of Supervision 
and Regulation dated April 9, 1973, the Board considered the staff 
recommendation to propose mortgage guaranty insurance as a permissible 
activity for bank holding companies. 
Indiana National Corporation, 
Indianapolis, Indiana, and First Virginia Bankshares Corporation, Falls 
Church, Virginia, had applications pending before the Board to engage 
in underwriting real estate mortgage guaranty insurance. Attached to 
the memorandum was a notice of proposed rulemaking. 
After discussion, the Board agreed that the wording of the 
notice of proposed rulemaking would be changed to include the stipulation 
that the name of the proposed subsidiary engaging in underwriting mortgage 
guaranty insurance may not resemble that of the holding company or any 
subsidiary bank, and that in no event may the resources of any banking 
subsidiary of the holding company be used to support such company if it 
encountered financial difficulties. 
The Board then approved issuance for comment of the notice of 
proposed rulemaking. A copy is attached as Item No. 15. 
Discount rate. On May 8, 1973, the Board had considered actions 
by the directors of the Federal Reserve Banks of Cleveland and San Francisco 
to raise the discount rate from 5-3/4 to 6 per cent. The Board members 

, 1581 
5/10/73 
-7-
had agreed in principle to approve the increases, but they had decided 
to defer action until today in order to allow time for additional Federal 
Reserve Banks to consider establishing a 6 per cent rate. 
The staff reported that four additional Banks had taken action 
to raise the rate to 6 per cent, including the Federal Reserve Bank of 
Boston on May 9 and the Federal Reserve Banks of New York, Chicago, 
and St. Louis earlier today. Further actions to establish a 6 per cent 
rate were expected from other Banks during the day. 
Following a staff summary of the latest economic and financial 
developments, the Board reviewed various timing considerations and 
decided that it would be desirable to approve and announce a 6 per cent 
rate today. 
Thereupon, the Board approved by unanimous vote the establishment 
of the following rates by the Federal Reserve Banks of Boston, New York, 
Cleveland, Chicago, St. Louis, and San Francisco, effective May 11: 6 per 
cent on advances to and discounts for member banks under section 13 and 13a 
of the Federal Reserve Act; 6-1/2 per cent on advances to member banks 
under section 10(b) of the Act; and 8 per cent on advances to individuals, 
partnerships, and corporations other than member banks under the last 
paragraph of section 13 of the Act. 
It was understood that the rate on 
any loans to nonmember banks granted in special circumstances resulting 
from implementation of earlier changes in Regulation J would be made at 
6 per cent.

(2) that a majority of the shares of capital stock is held and owned

interest in which is owned by citizens of the United States, chartered 
under the laws of the United States or of a State of the United States 
or by firms or companies the controlling interest in which is owned 
by citizens of the United States: and (3) that of the authorised capi-
tal stock specified in the Articles of Association at least 25 per 
cent has been paid in in cash and that each shareholder has individ-
ually paid in in cash at least 25 per cent of his stock subscription. 
Thereafter, the treasurer or secretary shall certify to the payment 
of the remaining instalments as and when each is paid in, in accord-
ance with law.

The Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve System 
grants its permission to The Huntington National Bank of Columbus 
("IINB") , Columbus, Ohio, pursuant to the provisions of Section 25 
of the Federal Reserve Act, to establish a branch in Georgetown, 
Grand Cayman, Cayman Islands, and to operate and maintain such 
branch subject to the provisions of such Section and of Regulation M. 
In granting this permission, it is understood that the 
branch will be used for developing new international business and 
not as a means of shifting loans or deposits from offices in the 
United States. 
It is also understood that there is co be no contact 
with the local public at the branch, and that its quarters, staff, 
and bookkeeping may, at least in part, be supplied under contract by 
another party. 
In view of the special character of this operation, the 
foregoing permission is granted subject to (1) a continuing review 
of these types of facilities, and after due notice may be withdrawn 
or modified, (2) the condition that reserves shall be maintained 
against deposits in this branch under Section 213.7 of Regulation M 
without benefit of the deduction provided in subparagraph (ii) of 
Section 213.7(a) or the exclusion provided in proviso (1) of Sec-
tion 213.7(b), and (3) the further condition that adequate informa-
tion covering the branch's operations will be maintained at your 
head office and will be available to the Board and its accredited 
representatives. 
The Board should be promptly informed of any 
modification in the branch's methods of operation, including changes 
in any contract under which services are supplied to the branch. 
Permission to establish the proposed branch is also 
granted subject to the condition that HNB shall liquidate its Nassau 
branch within six months of opening the Georgetown branch.

Under Section 19 of the Federal Reserve Act, deposits pay-
able only at a branch of a member bank located outside of the States 
of the United States and the District of Columbia are exempt from any 
limitations on rates of interest prescribed in Section 19 and Regu-
lation Q. Also, deposits at such branches are not generally subject 
to the reserve requirements of Regulation D. The general purpose, of 
these exemptions is to allow foreign branches of United States banks 
to compete abroad with foreign banks. 
In these circumstances, it lias 
long been and continues to be the policy of the Federal Reserve System 
to discourage deposits by United States residents at foreign branches 
of United States banks unless such deposits have been placed to serve, 
a definite, necessary purpose in connection with foreign or inter-
national transactions outside the United States. 
With respect to the establishment of foreign branches, funds 
provided by home office (whether in the form of allocated capital, ad-
vances, or otherwise) should be regarded as foreign assets for purposes 
of the foreign credit restraint effort. 
In view of the Board's concern 
with minimizing the impact of foreign investments on the balance of 
payments, the above consent is given on the condition that in the con-
duct of the branch's operations maximum reliance will be placed on 
resources obtained outside the United States. Accordingly, it is 
understood that any investment required will be made within the For-
eign Credit Restraint Guidelines as they apply to your bank and af-
filiates .

Barnett Banks of Florida, Inc., Jacksonville, Florida, a bank 
holding company within the meaning of the Bank Holding Company Act, has

1842(a)(3)) to acquire 80 per cent or more of the voting shares of Bar-
n ett Bank of Sarasota, N. A., Sarasota, Florida ("Sarasota Bank"), a 
Proposed new bank. 
Notice of the application, affording opportunity for interested 
Persons to submit comments and views, has been given in accordance with 
§ 3(b) of the Act. The time for filing comments and views has expired, 
and the Board has considered the application and all comments received 
^ light of the factors set forth in § 3(c) of the Act (12 U.S.C. 1842(c)). 
Applicant controls 40 banks with aggregate deposits of $1 billion, 
*eprenenting approximately 7 per cent of the total deposits of commercial 
banks in the State and is the second largest banking organization in 
Florida.

company formations and acquisitions approved through April 15, 1973,

a Proposed new bank, consummation of the proposal would not increase

The Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve System today 
approved actions by the directors of the Federal Reserve Banks of 
Boston, New York, Philadelphia, Cleveland, Richmond, Atlanta, Chicago, 
St. Louis, Minneapolis, Dallas and San Francisco increasing the discount 
rate of those banks from 5-3/4 per cent to 6 per cent, effective 
Friday, May 11. 
The action was in recognition of increases that have already 
t a k e n place in other short-term Interest rates and is intended to bring 
the discount rate into better alignment with short-term rates generally. 
The discount rate is the rate charged member banks for 
borrowings from their district Federal Reserve Banks.
