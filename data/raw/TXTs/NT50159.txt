Application to make foreign investment (Item No. 1). Unanimous 
approval was given to a letter to Manufacturers Hanover International 
Banking Corporation, New York, New York, consenting to its acquisition 
of additional shares of Commercial Export Credit Company Ltd., London, 
England. A copy is attached as Item No. 1. 
Mr. Poundstone then withdrew. 
Outside space. Mr. Shepardson commented on negotiations to 
terminate the current lease for space at the Architect Building in 
Rosslyn, Virginia, and, for reasons he mentioned, recommended that an 
effort be made to arrange a settlement of the matter for $25,000, with 
the understanding that, if necessary, settlement be made for a figure 
a s high as $27,940. He stated, in response to a question, that it had 
not proven feasible for the owners of the Watergate Office Building to 
take over the remaining portion of the Rosslyn lease. 
At the suggestion of Chairman Martin, Governor Sherrill and Mr. 
Shepardson were authorized to dispose of the matter if a settlement 
could be agreed upon that was satisfactory to them. 
Secretary's Note: Termination of the lease 
for $25,000 was subsequently agreed upon, 
and payment was transmitted to Architect 
Building Associates with a letter dated 
October 18, 1967.

Gentlemen: 
As requested in your letter of September 27, 1967, the 
Board of Governors grants consent for your Corporation to purchase 
and hold approximately 250,000 additional shares of Commercial 
Export Credit Company Ltd. ("CEC"), London, England, at a cost of 
approximately US$700,000, provided such shares are acquired within 
°ne year from the date of this letter. In this connection, the 
Board also approves the purchase and holding of shares of CEC in 
excess of 10 per cent of your Corporation's capital and surplus. 
The foregoing consent is given with the understanding 
that the investment now being approved, combined with other foreign 
loans and investments of your Corporation and Manufacturers Hanover 
International Finance Corporation will not cause the total of such 
loans and investments to exceed the guidelines established under 
the voluntary foreign credit restraint effort now in effect and 
that due consideration is being given to the priorities contained 
therein. 
Very truly yours, 
(Signed) Elizabeth L. Carmichael 
Elizabeth L. Carmichael, 
Assistant Secretary.
